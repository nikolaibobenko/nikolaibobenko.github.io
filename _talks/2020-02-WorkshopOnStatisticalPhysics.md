This year was the first time I participated in the annual [NCCR SwissMAP](https://www.nccr-swissmap.ch/) Workshop on Mathematical Physics held in Les Diablerets. [Here](http://www.unige.ch/math/mpseminar/Events/Diablerets2020/diablerets2020.html) is the conference website.

Of particular interest to me was a [talk](http://www.unige.ch/math/mpseminar/Events/Diablerets2020/slides/Expose.pdf) by Béatrice de Tilière on Elliptic Dimer models. The work has since been [published](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2007.14699.pdf) in an absolute show of force of a paper. It brings together many lingering results integrating them under one roof. In particular Fock's elliptic weights are shown to be Kasteleyn for certain kinds of infinite graphs giving us good graph immersions and the ability to analyse the resulting probabilistic model.
