I just returned from the annual Workshop on Statistical Physics in Les Diablerets. Of course due to COVID restrictions it was held with very few in place participants which is regrettable but also allowed for some nice collaboration and intimate lectures with helpful discussions.

The topics discussed covered nodal domains of solutions to the Helmholtz equation and a probabilitic version of that given by appropriate Gaussian fields. For centered, stationary, continuous Gaussian fields defined by their covariance function $$\kappa(x) = \mathbb{E}[f(0)f(x)]$$ one is interested in the question of percolation of the nodal domains $$D_l = \{f(x) \geq -l\}$$. For	 positive $$\kappa$$ the [FKG inequality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FKG_inequality) holds giving us access to many techniques from classical percolation theory. As an example it has been [shown](https://hal.archives-ouvertes.fr/hal-01634286/file/critical_threshold_BF.pdf) recently by Rivera and Vanneuville that the critical level $$l_c := \inf\{l\in\mathbb{R}: \text{a.s. }\exists \text{ infinite component in }D_l\}$$ satisfies $$l_c = 0$$ in two dimensions for the Bargman-Fock field given by 
$$\kappa(x) = e^{-\frac{|x|^2}{2}}$$. This was expected due to duality arguments and has now been shown rigorously along with specific sharpness results.

Other talks included a fantastic overview over the theory of Harnack curves given by Grigory Mikhalkin which was of particular interest to me as those show up as spectral curves of dimer models on $$\mathbb{Z}^2$$ periodic graphs with periodic weights. Of note was also a talk by Stas Smirnov on a current project on [diffusion limited aggregation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion-limited_aggregation). I plan to write a little overview on this soon.
